List the body systems affected by alcohol and briefly describe the commonly encountered pathologic effects in each 

Nervous system 

Impaired judgement and coordination, amnesia, encephalopathy, headache, fatigue, cognitive changes 

 

GI system 

Inflammation of esophagus, stomach, intestines, bleeding, vomiting, pancreatitis, impaired gluconeogenesis with decreased glucagon and increased lactate production thus decreasing fatty acid oxidation and increasing fat in liver cells, hepatitis/cirrhosis 

 

Cardiovascular system 

A small amount of ethanol decreases BP by reducing myocardial contractility. However, blood pressure increases after multiple drinks with unexplained arrhythmias. Increased LDL increases risk of heart disease. 

 

Genitourinary system 

Males- testicular atrophy, lower sperm count 

Females- Infertility, decreased ovarian size, fetal damage in pregnant 

 

Hematopoietic system 

Increased RBC size, hypersegmented neutrophils, hyperplastic bone marrow, decreased WBC production, anemia 

 

Respiratory system 

Increased risk of pneumonia, TB, acute respiratory distress syndrome 

 

Endocrine 

Increased cortisol, decreased T4 and T3 thyroid hormones 

 

Musculoskeletal 

Muscle weakness, changes in calcium metabolism, lower bone density 

 

Describe the common causes of injury to hepatocytes, their mechanisms, and the resulting histologic pattern of damage 

 

Alcohol 

-Large amounts of alcohol enhance lipolysis (breakdown of triglyceride into free fatty acids) through direct stimulation of the adrenal-pituitary axis.  

-Chronic alcohol consumption inhibit the oxidation of these fatty acids in the liver and inhibits the export of triglycerides as LDLs, causing them to build up in the liver. This causes fats to be collected in hepatocytes, hence fatty liver disease. Fats impede hepatocyte function.  

-An accumulation of bile acid inside the liver can occur due to malfunctioning hepatocytes, which leads to further damage.  

-Fatty hepatocytes also result in inflammation, leading to direct damage from the immune system.  

-Ethanol is broken down into acetaldehyde, which covalently binds to a variety of proteins, thereby altering liver function and structure to cause damage. 

-A minor metabolic pathway involves CYP2E1 (a type of CYP450) which can produce reactive oxygen species to damage hepatocytes. 

 

Note that with impeded liver function, protein synthesis by the liver is impeded which will lead to decreased oncotic pressure and hence edema.  

 

Other 

-Viral hepatitis 

-Gall stones from high cholesterol 

-Cancer 

 

Describe the consequences to liver structure and function of both acute and chronic alcohol exposure 

The earliest stage of ALD is steatosis (fatty liver), where excessive fat accumulates in liver cells and impairs liver function. Heavy drinking can cause this to progress to alcoholic hepatitis, where liver cells begin to be destroyed and the liver is inflamed. This then leads to cirrhosis with the replacement of normal liver tissue with non-living scar tissue.  

 

Cirrhosis leads to portal hypertension, leading to ascites and hepatic encephalopathy.
