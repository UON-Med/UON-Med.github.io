Describe common pathologic processes affecting the endocrine system (atrophy, hyperplasia, malignancy) 

Major disorders of the endocrine system: 

-Acromegaly (enlarged bones of hands, feet, jaw) 

-Addison's disease (hypersecretion from adrenal medulla) 

-Cretinism (congenital hypothyroidism) 

-Dwarfism (hyposecretion of growth hormone from pituitary, can be due to tumor, infection, trauma, genetic) 

-Gigantism (hypersecretion of somatotropic hormones causing excessive growth of long bones) 

-Grave's disease (autoimmune hyperthyroid) 

-Hyperparathyroidism (hypercalcemia) 

-Hypoparathyroidism (decreased secretion of parathyroid hormones, causes tetany) 

-Hypoglycemia due to excessive insulin production 

-Hashimoto's hypothyroidism (insufficient thyroxine production) 

 

-A malignancy of an endocrine organ often causes hypersecretion of a hormone. 

-Hyperplasia and hypertrophy often occurs to endocrine organs which are under strain to produce more hormones eg pancreatic hyperplasia due to high sugar in blood. Hyperplasia can also be a response to certain viral infections which interfere with cell proliferation regulation. Pathologic hyperplasia promotes the likelihood of cancerous proliferation. 

-Atrophy is a reduction in the size of an organ or tissue, and can be due to decreased workload, ischemia from gradual occlusion, inadequate nutrition, loss of endocrine stimulation, or pressure ie from a tumor.

Briefly describe the causes of dysfunction of the thyroid 

Hyperthyroidism: Graves disease or Hashimoto's (autoimmune), adenoma 

Hypothyroidism: Hashimoto's (autoimmune), iodine deficiency, granulomatous thyroiditis triggered by viral infection
