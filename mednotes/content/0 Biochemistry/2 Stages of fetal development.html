Define the following terms. 

Gamete: A haploid cell which fuses with another haploid cell during fertilisation in organisms that sexually reproduce 

Zygote: A fertilised egg (after egg and sperm combine) 

Embryo: The unborn offspring from 4th day to end of 8th week into pregnancy 

Fetus: Unborn offspring from end of 8th week to the moment of birth 

 

Endoderm: innermost primary germ layer of the embryo, gives rise to epithelium of the respiratory tract (larynx, pharynx [includes glands], trachea, lungs), the gastrointestinal tract except mouth and anus, the urinary bladder, the vagina, the urethra 

Mesoderm: middle layer, gives rise to connective tissue, muscle, cartilage, bone (including spine), bone marrow, lymphoid tissue, blood; and to the epithelia of blood + lymphatic vessels, body cavities, kidneys, ureters, gonads, genital ducts, pericardium (heart membrane), peritoneum (lines abdominal cavity and covers abdominal organs) 

Ectoderm: outside layer, gives rise to hair, skin, nails, lens of eye; nervous tissue; and the epithelia of sense organs, the nasal cavity, the sinuses, the mouth (including tooth enamel), the anal canal 

 

The major developmental events of each trimester of pregnancy: 

First Trimester 

-The development of pregnancy is counted from the first day of the woman's last menstrual period 

-Zygote travels through fallopian tube toward the uterus, and is called a morula as it divides into cells 

-Morula becomes a blastocyst and imbeds into the uterine lining 

-Week 3 development of brain, spinal cord, heart, gastrointestinal tract 

-Week 4/5 arm and leg buds develop, heartbeat at steady rhythm, placenta begins to form and produces important hormones including hCG, movement of rudimentary blood through main vessels, early structures that will become eyes and ears start forming 

-Week 6 formation of lungs, jaw, nose, palate begin, hand and feet buds have webbed structures, brain continues to form 

-Week 7 all essential organs have begun to form, eyelids and tongue form, hair and nipple follicles begin forming 

-Week 8 ear continuing to form, bones beginning to form, muscles can contract, facial features develop 

-Weeks 9 to 13 genitalia have formed, end of embryonic development 

 

Mother experiences mood swings and exhaustion, morning sickness, an increase in breast size and tenderness, decrease in sexual desire, cramping 

 

Second trimester 

-Weeks 14 to 16 fetus skin is transparent and fine hair (lanugo) forms on the head, fetus begins sucking and swallows bits of amniotic fluid, fingerprints form, meconium (earliest stool) builds up in fetus' intestinal tract to become first bowel movement, sweat glands develop, liver and pancreas produce fluid secretions, fetus moves around and may cause flutters to be felt by the mother 

-Weeks 17 to 20 more movements felt by mother, eyebrows and eyelashes grow in, nails begin to grow, Vernix begins to be produced to cover baby's skin from amniotic fluid, fetal heartbeat can be monitored by stethoscope 

-Weeks 21 to 23 lanugo covers fetus' entire body, fat begins to develop, all components of eye develop, liver and pancreas develop completely 

-Weeks 24 to 26 fetus develops sleep cycles noticeable by mother, fetus has startle reflex, air sacs form in lungs, nervous system develops and can control some functions 

 

Physical symptoms present in first trimester reduce for mother, but Braxton-Hicks (painless) contractions are begun to be felt. These increase in frequency over time. Mother visits bathroom more often and her heart rate increases. She begins to have heartburn. 

 

Third trimester 

-Weeks 27 to 32 fetus stores fat, rhythmic breathing movements occur, bones fully develop, fetus stores own calcium, iron and phosphorus  

-Weeks 33 to 36 fetus descends into head-down position, lanugo hair disappears from skin 

-Weeks 37 to 40 more movement felt, fingernails have grown long, all organs developed whilst lungs continue to mature, small breast buds on both sexes, delivery at any time 

 

Mother experiences discomfort due to rapid growth of uterus and continuous backache. This makes it difficult to sleep. Heartburn continues. Mood swings and exhaustion become very severe. Sharper, more painful contractions occur.
