Describe the roles of Ca2+ and PO43- in the body 

Ca2+ is needed for bone strength, muscle contractions, immune function, and epithelial cell regulation. 

PO43- is needed in nucleic acids, ATP production, buffer systems, and are complexed to proteins or lipids 

 

Describe the types and function of bone cells in the normal maintenance and turnover of the bony matrix 

Osteoblasts: form bony matrix and cause bone mineralisation 

Osteoclasts: degrade bony matrix and cause bone resorption 

 

The bones undergo constant remodeling, with bone mass maintained by the balanced activity of osteoblasts and osteoclasts. Remodeling is tightly regulated by local and endocrine factors, for example: 

-Estrogen, androgens and growth factors stimulate osteoblast formation; estrogen and calcitonin inhibit osteoclast formation 

-Thyroxine, vitamin A promote osteoclast formation; glucocorticoids inhibit osteoblast formation 

 

Describe the sources, manufacture and mechanisms of action of calcitonin, parathyroid hormone, and vitamin D in homeostasis of Ca2+ and PO43- 

 

Ca2+: 

 

PTH 

PTH is fast acting, and its release is dependent on serum Ca2+ concentration. PTH signals the bone to increase Ca2+ mobilisation/resorption, the kidney to increase Ca2+ reabsorption and production of active vitamin D. Active vitamin D is used by the small bowel to increase Ca2+ absorption from diet (increases expression of Ca2+ influx proteins at apical surface, basolateral sodium ATPase and NCX used) 

 

Calcitonin 

Calcitonin acts to decrease serum Ca2+ concentration. It inhibits osteoclasts and increases Ca2+ excretion in urine. It also decreases small bowel Ca2+ absorption from diet.  

 

Calcitriol 

Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) is produced in the skin from cholesterol due to UVR. Vitamin D2 is from plants. D2 and D3 are pro-hormones, which must be metabolised. D3 is metabolised into calcitriol. It is transported in the blood by the binding protein transcalciferin. Calcitriol exerts action by binding to intracellular receptors.  

 

Low calcitriol causes secondary hyperparathyroidism, rickets (bone deformities in children) and osteomalacia (weak bones in adults) 

 

PO43-: 

 

Phosphates are also stored in the bone. The kidney is the major regulator of phosphate balance through the action of secondary active Na+/PO43- cotransporters in the proximal tubule.  

 

FGF 23 

FGF 23 is a fibroblast growth factor secreted from bone in response to increased serum PO43- levels. It also decreases the renal expression of phosphate symporters. It also decreases calcitriol synthesis, decreasing Ca2+ and PO43- absorption from diet.  

 

PTH 

PTH causes the bone to release calcium and phosphorous, while signaling the kidney to increase calcitriol formation, increase excretion of phosphorous and decrease excretion of calcium.  

 

Calcitriol 

Increases phosphate absorption at small bowel.
