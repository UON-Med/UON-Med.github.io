Describe the types of point mutations (missense, nonsense, frameshift, mutations that affect splicing) 

A point mutation is a change in a single nucleotide.  

-Silent mutation: amino acid sequence not changed (multiple codons for each amino acid), but can impact rate of protein synthesis or affect splicing 
-Missense mutation: amino acid sequence changed 

-Nonsense mutation: stop codon produced, truncating the protein 

 

A frame shift can be caused by the addition or deletion of nucleotides (eg GAA AAC TTA read as GAA AAA CTT etc.) 

 

Note: splicing is the removal of introns (non-expressive DNA) from the mRNA prior to translation. Insertion, deletion, or change of more than 1 nucleotide at the mRNA splice site can result in RNA splicing mutations.  

-RNA splicing mutations include the introduction of the splice site upstream of original site, resulting in a truncated exon

Describe the additional factors that alter the expression/disease severity when a mutation is present 

-Move the threshold for trait expression, ie alter penetrance 

-Altering of dominance (from recessive to semi-dominant or conversely) 

-Shift the range of phenotypes/expressivity (to more or less severe) 

 

Note:  

-Expressivity = extent to which genotype shows as phenotype 

-Penetrance = proportion of population that possess genotype and expresses it as phenotype 

-Epistasis = the interaction between two or more genes to control a single phenotype 

 

Describe Mendelian inheritance patterns (autosomal dominant/recessive, X-linked and Y-linked) 

Autosomal inheritance: basic Punnett square 

X-linked: females (XX) are usually carriers, males (XY) will exhibit the phenotype. Males cannot pass it on to their sons. Can skip a generation eg hemophilia (poor blood clotting), red-green color-blindness 

Y-linked: only in males, and can only be passed from father to son. Females cannot be carriers.
