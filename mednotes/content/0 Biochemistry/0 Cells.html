Cells

1:55 PM 

Cells arise from other cells, hence acellular (things like viruses) cannot become cellular. Note that the majority of diseases are either prokaryotic cells or viruses 

 

Types of cells 

Eukaryotic- advanced, non-bacterial cells with organelles, slower reproduction 

Prokaryotic- unicellular, lack organelles, no nucleus, circular DNA just floating around (possesses flagellum) 

 

Parts of a cell 

Smooth ER: synthesis of steroidal hormones 

Rough ER: have ribosomes attached, used for protein synthesis 

Ribosomes: make very simple proteins 

Golgi apparatus: develop proteins from ribosomes and prepare them for transport both within the cell and outside the cell 

Lysosomes: digest used and un-needed protein, (where the fuck do these come from) including bacteria 

Mitochondria: possesses own genetic information, only passed through mother 

 

Phospholipid bilayer 

-Peripheral proteins are for communication and adhesion of cell membranes to other membranes 

-Integrated proteins serve as channels 

-Amphitropic proteins are not embedded and can be either floating around in the cytosol or as peripheral proteins 

-Carbohydrates are peripheral and are used for communication 

-Phosphorous head is attached to two fatty acid chains 

 

Fluid nature 

-No constituents within the membrane are bound to each other, hence the bilayer has a fluid nature. Phosphates have  polar interactions with water which fixes them in their positions. 

 

Transport membrane proteins 

Uniport: one type of solute, one way 

Symport: multiple types of solutes, one way 

Antiport: multiple types of solutes, both ways (Sodium potassium pump for nerves) 

 

 

 

 

 

Active transport is the movement of ions or molecules across a cell membrane into a region of higher concentration, assisted by enzymes and ATP. 

 

Further means of transport 

Endocytosis: acceptance of material into a cell 

Exocytosis: export of material out of a cell 

Phagocytosis: white-blood cells which engulf larger, solid particles eg bacteria 

Pinocytosis: endocytotic process in which liquid is engulfed and accepted into the cell 

Receptor-mediated endocytosis: cellular ingestion of specific ligand molecules that bind to receptors on the extracellular face of the plasma membrane of the cell 

 

Reproductive stuff 

The sperm possesses an acrosome is an organelle, derived from the golgi body, with a cap-like structure. It possesses digestive enzymes that break down the outer membrane of the oocyte.  

 

Defensive cells 

Leukocytes: white blood cells 

Goblet cells: secrete mucous to trap bacteria  

Skin cells: produce a physical barrier
