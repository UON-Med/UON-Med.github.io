Briefly describe the general mechanisms by which fluid can be lost from the intravascular space. 

Thirst is triggered by either low circulating blood volume (eg blood loss) or by the high concentration of electrolytes in the plasma. Water is lost through urine (controlled by vasopressin and RAAS), respiration, feces, sweat (controlled by sympathetic system)

Describe the role of the autonomic nervous system (sympathetic and parasympathetic) in blood pressure homeostasis via its actions on the heart and vasculature 

 

When arterial pressure falls, carotid sinus baroreceptors and aortic arch baroreceptors fire to the brain stem. An inhibition of cardiac vagal neurons and an activation of cardiac and vascular sympathetic neurons occurs from the brain stem. Cardiac vagal activity decreases; cardiac and vascular sympathetic activity increases. ACH release decreases, NA increases, HR increases, CO increases, arterial pressure increases. Likewise, NA release increases vasoconstriction which also increases arterial pressure. 

 

Note: the AVP (arterial vasopressin) system can also compensate for a decrease in arterial pressure. When the activity of baroreceptors decreases due to a fall in arterial pressure, the brain stem transmits signals to the hypothalamus. ADH is produced, and direct vasoconstriction via vasopressin receptors occurs. Vasopressin also causes increased fluid reabsorption in kidneys, leading to an increase in circulating blood volume and hence an increase in arterial pressure. 

 

When arterial pressure rises, the brain stem activates cardiac vagal neurons and inhibits cardiac + vascular sympathetic neurons. Cardiac vagal activity increases, ACH release increases, NA decreases, HR decreases. Vasoconstriction decreases due to decreases in NA, and thus arterial pressure falls.  

 

 

 

 

Define shock and briefly describe the physiologic consequences of inadequate tissue perfusion.  

 

 

Circulatory shock is the inadequacy of blood flow to tissues, that is often accompanied by hypotension. This is caused by insufficient cardiac output and/or low arterial blood pressure. Symptoms include pale and cold skin, tachycardia, hypotension, reduced urine output and the sensation of thirst. 

 

Inadequate tissue perfusion causes insufficient blood being delivered to the cells. This can cause cell damage and cell death.
