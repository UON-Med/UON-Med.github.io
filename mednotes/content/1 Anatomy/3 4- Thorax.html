List the structures that make up the thorax 

The thorax includes the thoracic cavity and the thoracic wall, which contain the heart, lungs, thymus gland, intercostal muscles. The mediastinum can be divided into superior and inferior mediastinum, via the sternal angle which is a horizontal plane passing through vertebrae T4 and T5.  

 

 

Structure of the heart 

 

 

The heart's arterial supply is provided by the coronary arteries. Venous drainage is through the coronary sinus and small veins that drain directly into the right atrium. 

 

Phrenic nerves 

The phrenic nerves arise from the cervical plexus, and supplies the skeletal muscles of the respiratory diaphragm and provides cutaneous innervation to the diaphragmatic pleura. A few fibers are distributed to the pericardium as well as the surrounding mediastinal and costal pleurae.  

 

Vagus nerves 

The vagus nerve has extensive distribution. Sensory fibers supply the epiglottis, the skin of the external auditory meatus and viscera of the head, neck, thorax and abdomen proximal to the left colic flexure. Motor fibers supply the intrinsic muscles of the larynx, pharynx, palate, as well as the smooth muscles of the thorax and heart. Secretomotor fibers supply the mucus glands of the larynx, pharynx, respiratory system and the digestive glands.  

 

Azygos system of veins 

These carry deoxygenated blood from the posterior abdomen and thorax to the superior vena cava.
