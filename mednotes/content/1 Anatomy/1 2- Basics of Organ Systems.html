In a sagittal view of the brain, identify the pituitary gland- in what bone does the pituitary gland rest? Name some of the hormones released by the pituitary gland and their actions at target organs. 

 

 

Hormones released by anterior pituitary: 

-Adrenocorticotropic hormone to regulate levels of the steroid hormone cortisol released from adrenal gland 

-Follicle stimulating hormone, stimulates growth of ovarian follicles that secrete hormones influencing the menstrual cycle, or stimulates sperm production 

-Growth hormone 

-Thyroid stimulating hormone causes thyroid gland to secrete T4 thyroxine, which is converted into active form T3 triiodothyronine responsible for metabolism 

-Luteinizing hormone triggers ovulation or stimulates the testes to secrete testosterone 

-Prolactin initiates and maintains milk secretion by the mammary glands, can cause erectile dysfunction in males through hypersecretion 

-Melanocyte stimulating hormone, role in skin pigmentation via production and distribution of melanin 

 

Hormones released by posterior pituitary: 

-ADH vasopressin retains water and constricts blood vessels 

-Oxytocin causes lactation and contraction of the womb during labor, present in men playing a role in production of testosterone by testes, also involved in sexual arousal, trust, anxiety 

 

Note: the pituitary is housed by the sphenoid bone. 

 

What does the pineal gland secrete? 

The pineal gland secretes melatonin which helps regulate other hormones and the body's circadian rhythm.  

 

What are the endocrine organs? 

-Pituitary 

-Thyroid (sits under larynx, at trachea) 

-Pineal 

-Adrenal 

-Pancreas secretes enzymes to further break down food, also secreting insulin and glucagon as required (sits under liver) 

 

What is the blood supply to the kidneys? 

Abdominal aorta to renal arteries, renal veins to inferior vena cava 

 

What do the adrenal glands do? 

-Aldosterone is secreted which conserves sodium, stimulates the secretion of potassium, and causes the bloodstream to reabsorb water to increase blood volume 

-Cortisol helps the body manage stress, converts protein into glucose, reduces inflammation, maintains blood pressure 

-Adrenaline is a vasoconstrictor, and causes the release of glucose from the liver (from glycogen) 

 

Is the stomach an endocrine organ? 

The enteric endocrine system, which includes the stomach, secretes gastrin to control gastric acid secretion, cholecystokinin in the small intestine that stimulates the secretion of pancreatic enzymes and bile, and secretin in the small intestine which stimulates the secretion of bicarbonate-rich fluids from the pancreas and liver. 

 

What is the blood supply to the reproductive organs? 

The penis receives three pairs of arteries, which branch off the internal pudendal artery; these are the artery to the bulb, the deep artery of the penis and the dorsal artery of the penis. The penis is drained by the accompanying veins to these arteries, but primarily by the deep dorsal vein. These veins return to the internal pudendal veins.  

 

The vasculature of the external female genitalia is primarily supplied by the internal pudendal arteries, which are branches of the anterior division of the internal iliac artery. 

 

What is the blood supply to the thyroid gland? 

The thyroid is supplied by the superior thyroid artery, a branch of the external carotid artery; and the inferior thyroid artery, a branch of the thyrocervical trunk. The superior and middle thyroid veins drain into the internal jugular veins, whilst the inferior thyroid vein drains into the brachiocephalic vein.
