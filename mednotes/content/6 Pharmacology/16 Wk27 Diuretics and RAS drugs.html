Describe the mechanism of action of commonly used diuretics: thiazides, loop diuretics, and potassium-sparing diuretics 

 

Angiotensin II acts on the AT1 receptor to: 

-Directly constrict blood vessels 

-Stimulate sympathetic action 

-Stimulate aldosterone release 

 

There are two main drugs used to block the effect of RAAS: 

-ACE inhibitors block ANGII formation and increase bradykinin (vasodilator) levels by reducing its breakdown 

-ARBs (Angiotensin receptor blockers) block AT1 receptors and can be helpful in reducing side effects of ACEi eg cough from bradykinin buildup 

 

Diuretics 

Site 1- proximal convoluted tubule: not commonly targeted 

 

Site 2- Loop of Henle: furosemide [K+ wasting, hypokalemia leads to arrhythmias] 

 

Site 3- early distal convoluted tubule: thiazide [K+ wasting] 

 

Site 4- cortical collecting duct: aldosterone inhibitor (aldactone) [K+ sparing]
