Describe the drugs used to treat anemia, acid-base balance, hyperkalemia, osteodystrophy, and fluid overload/hypertension 

 

Anemia 

Synthetic erythropoietin is used as an agonist on Epo receptors to increase RBC production.  

 

Acid-base balance 

NaHCO3 is given to restore bicarbonate levels and restore pH. 

 

Hyperkalemia 

-IV glucose and insulin stimulates glycogen synthesis and causes cellular K+ uptake 

-IV NaHCO3 corrects acidosis and cause K+ to move into cell through H+ exchange 

-Resin with Na+ and Ca2+ ions exchanged for K+ which passes into feces 

-Dialysis, diet 

-Avoid K+ sparing diuretics, ACEi, ATII antagonist 

 

Osteodystrophy 

-Dietary restriction of phosphate, and phosphate-binding agents for excretion in feces  

-Vitamin D supplementation 

-Ca2+ supplementation 

 

Describe the effect of NSAIDs on kidney function 

Prostaglandins inhibit NaCl reabsorption and inhibit ADH, thus causing the increased excretion of salts and water. This is an important counter-regulatory mechanism against the RAAS. NSAIDs block these actions of prostaglandins, resulting in salt and water retention. Chronic NSAID use leads to nephropathy and progressive renal failure.
