List the clinical indications (broad categories of illness) for treatment with corticosteroids 

-Replacement therapy in adrenal failure 

-In combination with cytotoxic drugs in treating malignancies 

-As an immunosuppressant/anti-inflammatory 

 

Glucocorticoids reduce phospholipase A2 activation and inhibit COX.  

 

Describe the unwanted effects of exogenous glucocorticoids and identify steps that may be taken to reduce them 

High cortisol leads to Cushing's syndrome: with dangerous buildup of visceral fat. Cortisol also inhibits gonadal steroid hormones and causes suppressive effects on osteoblasts. This leads to osteoporosis. Growth retardation can also occur, even if small doses are used. Metabolic dysfunction will also occur.  

 

High aldosterone also causes fluid retention, hypertension and edema.  

 

Describe the complications of prolonged treatment with corticosteroids resulting in physiological dependence (compare to drugs of abuse and psychological dependence) 

-Adrenal insufficiency in sudden withdrawal  

-Immunosuppression 

-Cushing's syndrome 

-Osteoporosis 

-Peptic ulcer disease 

-Growth retardation  

 

Describe the effect that routes of administration can have on possible side effects of a medication 

Route of administration can limit the systemic exposure of glucocorticoids, eg: 

-Inhalation for asthma 

-Topical for eczema 

-Intranasal for rhinitis 

 

Adrenal insufficiency can still occur with withdrawal.
