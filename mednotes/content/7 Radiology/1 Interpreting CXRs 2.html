5 Shades of gray 

  1. 1.Air 

  2. 2.Fat 

  3. 3.Soft tissue or fluid 

  4. 4.Bone 

  5. 5.Metal 

Body parts are identified by examining the contact between different shades of gray. Fluid within soft tissue cannot be identified, as they are of the same shade of gray/density.  

 

PA vs AP 

A PA CXR is used for mobile patients that are able to stand. This can estimate cardiac size, and produces higher overall quality. An AP CXR is of much less quality, used for less mobile patients. It produces an exaggerated heart size.  

 

In taking the image, the patients is at the detector, between the detector and X-ray source. PA has the patient hugging the detector, whilst in AP the patient faces the opposite way. In AP, the scapulae produce significant obstruction to the image of the thorax.
