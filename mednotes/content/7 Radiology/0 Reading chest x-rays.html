Types of scans, and the reason X-rays are so useful 

X-ray: cheap, readily available, low radiation 

Ultrasound: user-dependent, readily available, no radiation 

CT: tones of radiation, 3D view of anatomy, not readily available 

MRI: limited resource, takes a long time to take

Mark's technique 

1, Lungs- top, middle, bottom, left/right, pleural spaces, airways 

2, Heart and mediastinum- location, size and contour, hila, vessels (left and right) 

3, Bones- vertebrae, ribs, clavicles 

4, Extras- lines, tubes, leads, devices 

5, Review areas- lung apices, retrocardiac, costophrenic angles
