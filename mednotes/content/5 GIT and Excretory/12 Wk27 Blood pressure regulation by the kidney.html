Describe the role of the kidney in blood pressure control, and integrate the kidney's role with the role of the heart, blood vessels and ANS 

The kidneys provide a long-term means of controlling blood pressure, by regulating the excretion of Na+ and hence H2O. Mechanoreceptors at the carotid sinus, aortic arch and atria control the tone of vascular smooth muscle to control blood pressure. The ANS responds to increases or decreases in oxygen demand, and causes vasodilation or vasoconstriction; and action at the heart.  

 

Describe the role of the kidney in volume overload due to cardiac dysfunction 

In cardiac hypertrophy causing chronic hypertension, the kidneys may excrete an excessive amount of fluid and Na+ from a tiny increase in blood pressure.  

 

Note that osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus release ADH to promote thirst in excess fluid loss. 

 

Describe the cardiovascular consequences of volume overload eg due to renal dysfunction or exogenous fluid 

Increased cardiac work will lead to hypertrophy and dysfunction. Atherosclerosis will also worsen. Edema may result.
