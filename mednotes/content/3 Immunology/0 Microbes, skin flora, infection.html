List the classes of microbes and briefly describe the features of each 

-Virus: consists of genetic material, protein coat (capsid), envelope (lipid and protein, obtained from host cell), cannot replicate outside of host cell. Viruses are classed based on their genome structure.  

-Bacteria: prokaryote with two main types- gram positive and gram negative. Peptidoglycan (ester-linked) cell wall, targeted by antibiotics, lack membrane-enclosed organelles, DNA in the cytoplasm instead of nucleus, divide by asexual "binary fission" 

-Archaea: have more complex RNA polymerases than bacteria, do not contain peptidoglycan, exist in extremely harsh environments, unique metabolism through methanogenesis, reproduce asexually through binary fission, budding and fragmentation 

-Protozoa: eukaryotes that are single-celled, either heterotrophic or autotrophic, have animal-like cell membrane, plant-like cell walls (or covered in pellicle), motile via cilia, flagella or pseudopodia 

-Parasites 

-Fungi: eukaryotes, mostly multicellular, have a cell wall, lack movement 

 

Define microbiota and microbiome 

Microbiota are normal bacterial flora which are essential to health, living in our bodies without causing disease. They assist in the normal development of the immune system, in nutrient production and in the prevention of infection through competition with pathogenic microbes. Microbiota refers to the microbial species found in an ecosystem.  

 

Microbiome refers to the genomes of the microbes in an ecosystem.

The skin and skin flora provide a physical and bacterial barrier that prevents the entry of pathogenic microbes. 

Skin structural elements, excretions (like sweat), and anti-microbial peptides produced by bacteria protect the body.  

 

Commensalism vs pathogenic 

Commensals can cause disease if the skin barrier is disrupted. Furthermore, opportunistic foreign non-microbiota microbes can cause disease if the barrier is breached, or if skin conditions change.
