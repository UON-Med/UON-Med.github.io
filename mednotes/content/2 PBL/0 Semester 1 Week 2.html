Briefly describe the common methods used to determine whether a pregnancy is progressing normally 

First trimester:  

-Fetal ultrasound 

-Maternal blood testing 

 

Second trimester:  

-Blood tests for multiple markers, looking for genetic defects 

 

Other tests: 

-Chorionic villus sampling: placenta tissue used to test for chromosomal abnormalities and certain other genetic problems 

-Amniocentesis: amniotic fluid used to diagnose genetic defects or fetal health problems such as infection 

-Cell-free fetal DNA test: investigates for genetic defects by extracting fetal DNA from the maternal bloodstream 

-Percutaneous umbilical cord sampling:  withdrawing a small sample of the fetal blood from the umbilical cord 

 

Describe how ultrasound works and why it is the imaging technique of choice in pregnancy 

Ultrasound scanning emits high-frequency sound waves through the uterus. These bounce off objects in the uterus, and the echoes are turned into an image on a screen. These images refresh to produce a video. They are the technique of choice as they produce no harmful radiation, and provide a clear view of the baby. They also provide enough detail for the screening of any defects or diseases.
