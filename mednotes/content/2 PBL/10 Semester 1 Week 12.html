Briefly describe the questions asked in a respiratory review of system 

-Any cough, sputum or blood? 

-Any shortness of breath or wheezing? 

-Any apnea/loud snoring? 

-Fever/night sweats? 

-Any showings on a recent CXR? 

-Any lumps, bleeding, masses or discharge from the breast? 

 

Describe the clinical presentation and course of COPD 

COPD: persistent respiratory symptoms and airflow limitation due to airway/alveolar abnormalities. Symptoms of COPD usually occur over the age of 40. COPD can progress in stages, all the way to end-stage COPD where patients pass away.  

 

Compare the CXR images of emphysema and chronic bronchitis 

In emphysema, bilaterally hyper-lucent (very dark space) lungs of large volume due to thin peripheral vascular markings. Chronic bronchitis produces pronounced vasculature on a CXR.  

 

Describe PET scanning, and the unique function information that can be obtained from them 

A very small dose of a radiotracer is injected, which releases gamma radiation read by a PET scanner. A 3D image is provided, and the results reveal the cellular level metabolic changes occurring in an organ or tissue eg blood flow, oxygen use, glucose metabolism etc. The scan can be used to both diagnose problems and assess the efficacy of treatment plans. A PET scan is useful as it can be used to look for diseases at an early stage before architectural changes develop. Radiation from a PET scan is similar to that from a CT.
