Class questions 

-CF related diabetes: 

Thick, sticky mucus scars the pancreas and stops it from producing normal amounts of insulin. Furthermore, patients may not respond to insulin in the right way.  

-CF related liver disease: 

Small bile ducts become blocked by mucus. The surround liver becomes damaged and scarred. This spreads throughout the liver. 

-CF related GI disease: 

The GI tract becomes covered in sticky mucus, leading to malabsorption. It also leads to damage and scarring by harboring infection. 

-CF related heart disease: 

High pulmonary pressure causes right sided heart failure.  

-CF related musculoskeletal disease: 

Malabsorption leads to decreased bone density and functionality.  

-CF related renal disease: 

Stress incontinence related to coughing, sneezing, laughing and lifting. High calcium levels lead to kidney stones.  

-Palms of hands: 

Aquagenic wrinkling of the palms, as a salt imbalance in the skin cells leads to increased water retention. 

-High risk organisms: 

P aeruginosa affects 30-80% of patients. S aureus affects 20-60% of patients. H influenza affects up to 35% of patients. Other common ones include MRSA, S maltophilia, B cepacia 

-Reproductive disease: 

Cystic fibrosis causes vas deferens to be blocked with mucus. In females, fertilisation can be difficult due to thick mucus. 

-Tetralogy of Fallot: 

Congenital heart defect involving the classical 4 problems: a ventricular septal defect mixes blood, pulmonary stenosis, right ventricle hypertrophy, overriding aorta allowing blood to flow into aorta from both ventricles
