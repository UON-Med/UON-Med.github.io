Briefly describe the useful functions of pain 

-Makes us associate harmful actions with the sensation of pain, resultantly discouraging us from such actions 

-Alerts us to disease 

 

Define nociception and differentiate it from pain 

Nociception is the reception of potentially harmful stimuli by the nociceptors. This is often caused by damage to tissue. Signals are sent to the brain, where they are assessed. If they are dangerous, the brain creates the sensation of pain. Thus, nociception can occur without pain and pain can occur with no nociception.  

 

Note: mechanoreceptors detect touch and vibration, whilst thermoreceptors detect temperature. 

 

Briefly describe the assessment of clinical signs of acute inflammation

-Heat 

-Swelling 

-Pain

Briefly describe and compare the signs associated with systemic vs localised inflammation 

Systemic (chronic) inflammation is long-term, and can result from: 

-A failure to eliminate the cause of an acute inflammation 

-An autoimmune response 

-A chronic irritant of low intensity 

 

Localised (acute) inflammation starts rapidly and quickly becomes severe. It is usually resolved soon.

Briefly describe the 3 main components of an FBC (WBC and differential, RBC and indices, Platelets) 

The WBC count measures the number of white blood cells in the blood, and the WBC differential determines the percentage of each type of WBC present. 

 

An RBC count measures the number of red blood cells in the blood, and the indices include average red blood cell size (MCV), hemoglobin amount per red blood cell (MCH) and hemoglobin concentration of the red blood cells (amount of hemoglobin relative to cell size) (MCHC) 

 

A platelet count determines the number of platelets in the blood. An abnormally large count is considered thrombocytosis, and a low count is considered thrombocytopenia.

Cellulitis and its complications 

-Bacterial infection involving the inner layers of the skin (dermis and subcutaneous fat) 

-Legs and face are most common sites involved 

Complications 

-Severe cellulitis may cause the spread of bacteria into the bloodstream (sepsis) 

-Infected tissue may die (gangrene) 

 

Brief info on antibiotics 

-Target bacterial cells by identifying their differences to human cells (eg presence of cell wall, different mechanisms of DNA/protein synthesis) 

 

Side effects of antibiotics 

-Wipe out friendly bacterial cells too, possibly leading to more harm 

-Damage of mucosal areas 

-Skin sensitivity
