Briefly describe the range of symptoms that can be caused by changes to heart rate and rhythm 

Tachycardia reduces stroke volume by decreasing ventricular filling time. This will lead to fatigue and breathlessness due to excessive cardiac work. Furthermore, increased myocardial oxygen demand can lead to angina particularly in patients of coronary heart disease. Chronic states of tachycardia lead to systolic heart failure due to loss of contractility (dilation of ventricular muscle).  

 

Briefly describe the general mechanisms that disrupt normal cardiac conduction eg loss of structural integrity, ischemia, electrolyte imbalance, toxins and drugs 

-The loss of structural integrity in damaging intercalated discs will inhibit cardiac conduction. Hypertrophy and fibrosis can will cause a remodeling of the conducting pathway through the heart, disrupting normal conduction.  

-Ischemia causes a reduction in available ATP, and thus the Na+/K+ ATPase no longer functions. This causes cell depolarisation, reducing cardiac conduction velocity. 

-Electrolyte imbalance can impede the depolarisation and repolarisation of action potentials, inhibiting cardiac conduction. 

-Toxins and drugs can block certain ion channels and thus disrupt the action potential

Describe the uses of a UEC test 

This tests for kidney function and electrolyte imbalance. People on certain medications will require a UEC test eg people on diuretics need to watch for potassium loss.
