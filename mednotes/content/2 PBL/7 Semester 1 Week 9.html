Describe the key features of somatic, visceral and referred pain. 

-Somatic pain is caused by the activation of pain receptors in either the body surface or musculoskeletal tissues. Somatic pain is usually aggravated by activity and relieved by rest. 

-Visceral pain is pain resulting from damage to the internal organs, with the activation of nociceptors in the chest, abdomen or pelvic areas. Visceral pain is not well-localised and is usually described as pressure-like, deep squeezing, etc. The viscera (organs) are highly sensitive to stretch (distension), ischemia and inflammation, but relatively insensitive to things like cutting or burning. 

-Referred pain is pain perceived at a location other than the site of the painful stimulus/origin 

 

Compare the subjective experience of visceral vs somatic pain, including associated symptoms. 

-Somatic pain can be felt as pricking, sharp, burning or throbbing pain 

-Visceral pain can be felt as a dull ache, pressure-like; a deep squeeze

Define cardiac biomarker and briefly describe their use as markers of tissue damage 

Cardiac biomarkers are biomarkers used to evaluate heart function. A cardiac biomarker test measures the levels of enzymes and proteins that are linked with injury of the heart muscle. These include the enzyme Creatine Kinase A (CK), and the proteins troponin I and T.

Briefly describe the imaging techniques used for assessing coronary artery function: angiogram, CT angiography, and nuclear medicine studies (Note: stress test is the most common way to assess function) 

Angiogram 

-Requires a catheter to be placed into a blood vessel and guided to the area of the coronary vessel to be studied. There, an iodine dye (contrast material) is injected into the vessel. X-rays are used to develop a picture of this area for study, and are used to examine blood flow in a blood vessel. 

 

CT angiography 

-A contrast dye is injected throughout the blood stream and a CT scan is taken. This is essentially a CT scan of the coronary arteries 

 

Nuclear heart scan 

-A radioactive tracer is injected into a blood vessel and concentrates in the heart, and a gamma camera examines the heart 

-A nuclear stress test occurs when blood flow to the heart is maximised via exercise or a drug, and an x-ray or gamma camera is used to examine the heart as differences in blood flow to different parts of the heart are more obvious when the blood flow is increased. A gamma camera rotates around the patient to produce 3d images of the heart. 

 

GTN and Arginine 

GTN supplements blood concentrations of NO to vasodilate. Spray has a more rapid action and longer shelf life. Arginine turns into NO. 

 

Aspirin 

Aspirin reduces the effectiveness of platelets in forming blood clots. This helps the blood flow easily, reducing fever and inflammation.

Ischemia vs infarction at both clinical and molecular level 

Ischemia is treatable with medications and observations clinically, infarction is a medical emergency. At the molecular level, ischemia is caused by a narrowing of the blood vessels, and can involve either only plaque or with blockage by an embolus. An infarction involves a blockage which causes the local death of tissue.
