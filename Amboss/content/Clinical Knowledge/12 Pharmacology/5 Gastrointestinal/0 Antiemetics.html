Antiemetics

Abstract

Antiemetics are a heterogeneous group of drugs used to treat various causes of nausea and vomiting. Different antiemetics act on different receptors, and they may have a peripheral effect, a central effect, or both. Whereas serotonin agonists, for example, bind 5-HT3 receptors and effectively combat cytotoxic drug nausea, certain anticholinergic drugs target M2 receptors and specifically treat motion sickness (kinetosis). Hospitals and clinics commonly use the dopamine D2 antagonist metoclopramide. However, because of its strong central effect and possible extrapyramidal side effects, metoclopramide must be used with caution.

Prochlorperazine

 D2
  • Antiemetic effect at the area postrema
  • Antipsychotic agent
  • Used in severe hyperemesis gravidarum
  • Depression
  • Fatigue
  • Diarrhea
  • Hyperprolactinemia

Domperidone
  • Antiemetic effect at the area postrema
  • Prokinetic effect
  • Prokinetic effect: to treat diabetic and postsurgery gastroparesis (delayed gastric emptying)
  • Used in severe hyperemesis gravidarum

Metoclopramide
  • Antiemetic effect in the CNS (!) and at the area postrema
  • Prokinetic effect : Increased gastric contractions, duodenal and jejunal motility, resting tone of the lower esophageal sphincter and decreased pylorus sphincter activity allow food to pass more quickly through the stomach and the small intestine.

Indications

  • Nonspecific nausea and vomiting
  • Chemotherapy-induced nausea
  • Vertigo (e.g., vestibular neuritis, MÃ©niÃ¨re's disease)
  • Motion sickness
  • Gastrointestinal motility disorder (e.g., due to diabetic gastroparesis)
  • Postoperative nausea and vomiting

Contraindications

  • Dopamine receptor antagonists
    • Intestinal obstruction (ileus)
    • Prolactin-dependent tumors
    • Parkinson's disease
  • Serotonin receptor antagonists: severe liver disease, prolonged QT interval
  • Anticholinergic agents and antihistamines with anticholinergic side effects
    • Tachyarrhythmias, heart failure
    • Urinary retention, BPH
    • Glaucoma
    • Pyloric stenosis

We list the most important contraindications. The selection is not exhaustive.
